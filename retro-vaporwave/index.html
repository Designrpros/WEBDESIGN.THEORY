VAPORWAVE

A E S T H E T I C

// HISTORY

Vaporwave emerged in the early 2010s as a music genre and visual art movement that Sampling from 1980s and 1990s corporate Muzak, Japanese City Pop, and smooth jazz. The aesthetic reaction against the perceived soullessness of modern digital culture. It transforms nostalgic relics of late capitalism—Marlboro logos, Roman busts, Windows 95 icons—into dreamlike, melancholic collages. The visual language draws heavily from cyberpunk, retrowave, and glitch art, creating an alternate reality where the future never quite arrived.

// VISUAL ELEMENTS

GRID BACKGROUND

Perspective grids evoke 80s computer graphics and OutRun aesthetics

SUNSET GRADIENT

Pink to cyan gradients reminiscent of digital sunsets

RETROFUTURISM

Glitch text and neon colors capture an imagined past future

// DO'S & DON'TS

✓ DO

✦Use pink and cyan color combinations for maximum impact
✦Incorporate 80s/90s nostalgia elements and imagery
✦Add subtle glitch effects and animations
✦Use pixel fonts for headings and key text
✦Layer gradients with grid patterns for depth

✗ DON'T

◆Overwhelm with too many bright colors at once
◆Use realistic photography without stylization
◆Forget the melancholic, dreamy atmosphere
◆Apply the aesthetic without understanding its roots
◆Make it too clean—embrace the digital decay
